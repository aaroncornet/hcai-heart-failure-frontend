Serum Creatinine

A rise in blood creatinine concentration is a late marker, observed only with marked damage to functioning nephrons.

Diagnostic serum creatinine studies are used to determine renal function. The reference interval is 0.6–1.3 mg/dL (53–115 µmol/L). Measuring serum creatinine is a simple test, and it is the most commonly used indicator of renal function.
